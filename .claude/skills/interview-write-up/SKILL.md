# Interview Write Up

## Background

I'm frequently required to help with interviewing candidates for technical engineering roles at Zilch. These are 'culture fit' interviews, or hiring manager interviews. They precede technical rounds and focus on attitudes and behaviours over exploring deep technical ability.

This skill describes how to assist in drafting a write up.

## Instructions

You will synthesise a draft write-up of a candidate interview using feedback from the user, me, and a transcript or summary of the interview.

1. Ask me what my decision was (advance to the next stage, or reject) and what I took from the interview that brought me to that decision.
2. If anything is unclear or ambiguous, ask clarifying questions before proceeding.
3. Ask me for the transcript or summary of the interview. All our interviews are now automatically transcribed so I should be able to get a full transcript.
4. Determine the candidate's level. This may already be clear from conversation context
   (e.g. interview prep earlier in the session), or it may form part of my decision
   (when a candidate is being assessed for either level). If unclear, ask me.
   Read the corresponding crib sheet:
   - L1/L2: `resources/recruitment/l12-interview-crib-sheet.md`
   - L3/L4: `resources/recruitment/l34-interview-crib-sheet.md`
   Use this for context on what was being assessed and what good/weak answers look like.
5. Be alert to potential transcription errors, particularly with domain-specific terminology (e.g. "merchants" transcribed as "martians").
6. Challenge me if my assertions or impressions are not supported by the transcript. If the discrepancy stands after discussion, note it in the write-up.
7. Generate a write up synthesising my feedback with the transcript. Bear in mind the Format and Style guide below. Output the write-up directly to the conversation. No need to generate a file.

## Format and Style

- The ATS system accepts feedback as a single free text field with limited formatting. Stick to a single block of plain text.
- The write-up need not restate the advance/reject decision — that is captured separately in the ATS.
- Aim for four or five paragraphs.
- Keep the wording compact; avoid unnecessary padding and redundancy.
- Stay objective and rational; avoid subjective or emotional language such as "I liked him".

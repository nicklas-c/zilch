# Interview Guide: Yury Kitkevich

**Role:** Back-end L4 | **Location:** Vilnius (fully remote) | **Date:** 3 July 2026

---

## Describe two real projects from your past career: the one you were most enthusiastic about — where you couldn't wait to get to work on a Monday morning — and the one you were least enthusiastic about, where getting out of bed was a real drag.

- Does enthusiasm skew purely toward greenfield architecture/infrastructure, or does he light up about product impact too?
- 20 years at one company — has he had a "tedious" phase? What kept him there rather than leaving?
- If the tedious example is maintenance/operations work, probe hard — this role will have operational responsibility
- What does his answer reveal about what he's looking for at Zilch specifically?

---

## Your CV reads like an infrastructure architect at a consultancy firm. You're applying for a software engineering role at a product firm. What's driving the career pivot?

- Listen for self-awareness: does he recognise the gap himself, or does he resist the framing?
- Follow up: "What adjustments do you think you'll need to make?" — looking for him to identify product ownership, shaping the "what", earning credibility from scratch
- Thread into remote working naturally: "And how does being fully remote change the difficulty of that adjustment?"
- If he pushes back on the characterisation, test it — ask for a concrete example of him owning a business domain end-to-end, not just the platform beneath it
- Watch for: does he frame Zilch as a destination (wants product ownership) or an escape (leaving Exadel/consultancy)?

---

## Tell me about a time you identified a significant technical problem or opportunity that wasn't on anyone's roadmap, and drove it forward. How did you build the case, get buy-in, and see it through?

- The CDC filtering (~800k updates eliminated) and the Spark→Glue migration both look like candidates — push for the full story: whose roadmap was it not on? Who did he need to convince?
- Was this within a client engagement (where the "stakeholder" is the client paying for his services) or within Exadel itself? The dynamics are very different
- At L4 we expect cross-team scope — has he driven something that required influencing people outside his immediate team?
- Did he delegate or bring others along, or was he a lone architect handing down decisions?

---

## What advice would you give to an engineer who is not yet using AI to support their coding?

- No AI mentioned anywhere on the CV — start with understanding his actual usage before asking for advice
- If usage is minimal: is he curious and open, or dismissive?
- If usage is real: why didn't it feature on the CV? What has it changed about how he works?
- For an L4: has he thought about AI adoption at a team level, or only personal productivity?

---

## Reserve: What approaches or techniques do you employ to ensure the features you deliver are of high quality?

- Use if time permits or if earlier answers leave quality mindset unclear
- Probe whether his quality thinking extends beyond infrastructure reliability (uptime, scaling) into product correctness (business logic, edge cases, user impact)

# On-Call Noise Reduction

## Context

Zilch operates a 24/7 on-call rota staffed by DevOps and Platform engineers. Call-outs are triggered primarily by Datadog monitors routed through OpsGenie. The current volume of overnight call-outs is so high that on-call engineers report being unable to share a bedroom with their partners during on-call rotations — a significant quality-of-life issue.

The root cause is systemic: many teams across Zilch create Datadog monitors and routinely add the SRE integration without understanding that this results in OpsGenie pages to the on-call engineer. There is no standard approach to monitor ownership, priority classification, or notification routing. As a result, the on-call engineer is paged for low-priority alerts (e.g. P3 monitors) that do not require immediate action and could safely wait until business hours.

## Description

Reduce noisy and unnecessary call-outs to a level where on-call engineers can reasonably expect a good night's sleep. False positives and non-actionable pages should not outnumber genuine issues requiring immediate intervention.

This is a cross-team effort. DevOps owns the epic and will lead the initiative, but child tickets will be allocated to other teams where monitors or alert routing fall under their ownership.

## Success Criteria

* On-call engineers are not routinely woken overnight for alerts that do not require immediate action.
* The ratio of actionable to non-actionable out-of-hours pages is significantly improved (target: actionable pages are the majority).
